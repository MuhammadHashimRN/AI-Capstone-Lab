Grounding Justification: Why the Agent Needs Domain-Specific Data
===================================================================

The Dynamic Inventory Reorder Agent cannot rely solely on its pre-trained
knowledge (the LLM "brain") for the following reasons:

1. PRIVATE DATA: Supplier catalogs contain proprietary pricing, MOQs, lead times,
   and reliability scores that are specific to our company's supplier agreements.
   No pre-trained model has access to TechDistributors Inc's volume discount
   thresholds or PrimeParts Direct's defect rates. Without RAG grounding, the
   agent would hallucinate supplier information.

2. REAL-TIME INVENTORY: Current stock levels (e.g., SKU-001 has 45 units) change
   daily. The LLM's training data is months or years old and cannot reflect
   today's warehouse state. The agent must query live inventory data to make
   reorder decisions.

3. HISTORICAL DEMAND PATTERNS: Sales history is company-specific and includes
   seasonal patterns, promotional impacts, and return rates unique to our product
   mix. Generic "electronics demand" knowledge from pre-training would produce
   inaccurate forecasts for our specific SKUs.

4. PROMOTIONAL CALENDAR: Upcoming events like "Spring Sale" or "Black Friday"
   directly impact demand multipliers. These are internal business decisions that
   no external LLM could predict. The agent needs this grounded context to adjust
   order quantities proactively.

5. SUPPLIER PERFORMANCE HISTORY: Reliability scores and defect rates are based on
   our actual experience with each supplier. The multi-criteria supplier selection
   algorithm depends on this proprietary performance data, not generic vendor
   ratings.

In summary, the RAG pipeline transforms the agent from a generic assistant into a
domain expert with access to our company's specific operational data, enabling
accurate, actionable procurement decisions rather than hallucinated guesses.
